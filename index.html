Hi!

I am an applied machine learning researcher at NASA Ames. My main focus is on leveraging machine learning for astronomy and astrophysics applications.

My research has focused on contributing to expand the exoplanet science output of the survey missions Kepler and TESS by developing ML models to sift through these high-quality large datasets of transit signals for accelerating planet candidate vetting and exoplanet validation.

Other research interests include:

From more general...

  • Uncertainty quantification and characterization of deep learning models.
  • Model explainability (XAI) - What's inside the box?

...to more concrete applications:

  • Denoising JWST mid-infrared images by working on algorithms for cosmic ray detection with the goal to produce better quality data products down the road for all researchers using the data to advence their work and the output the community can get from NASA's current flagship observatory.
  • ML-based transit detection for TESS and Kepler.
